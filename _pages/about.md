I am an Associate Research Fellow at the Korea Institute of Finance(KIF).

My academic training is rooted in **Experimental Economics, Auction Theory, and Industrial Organization.** My research interests lie at the intersection of market design and public policy, with applications in public procurement, fiscal policy, and financial markets.

During my Ph.D., I developed a theoretical framework showing that, under budget constraints, optimal auction design may depart from standard auctions—which typically maximize revenue—and instead take non-standard forms, such as proportional auctions. This insight was supported by experimental evidence.

I also examined joint bidding in auctions and showed that it can generate pro-competitive effects that outweigh potential anti-competitive concerns, particularly in settings characterized by strong asymmetries among bidders. 

Before joining KIF, I spent four years at the Korea Institute of Public Finance (2020–2024), where I led research on public procurement policy, environmental taxation, and fiscal management. Since 2024, I have been working at KIF's Financial Consumer Research Division, focusing on consumer finance and payment systems, including digital assets such as stablecoins.
